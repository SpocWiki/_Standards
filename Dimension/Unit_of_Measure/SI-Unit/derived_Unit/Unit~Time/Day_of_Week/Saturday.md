## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Saturday** is the day of the week between Friday and Sunday. 
> 
> No later than the 2nd century, 
> the Romans named Saturday diēs Sāturnī ("Saturn's Day") for the god Saturn. 
> 
> His planet, Saturn, controlled the first hour of that day, according to Vettius Valens. 
> 
> The day's name was introduced into West Germanic languages, 
> and is recorded in the Low German languages such as Middle Low German satersdach, saterdach, Middle Dutch saterdag (Modern Dutch zaterdag), and Old English Sæternesdæġ, Sæterndæġ or Sæterdæġ.
>
