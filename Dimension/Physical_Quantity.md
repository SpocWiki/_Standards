## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> A physical quantity (or simply quantity) is a property of a material or system 
> that can be quantified by measurement. 
> 
> A physical quantity can be expressed as a value, 
> which is the algebraic multiplication of a numerical value and a unit of measurement. 
> 
> For example, the physical quantity mass, symbol m, can be quantified as m=n kg, 
> where n is the numerical value and kg is the unit symbol (for kilogram). 
> Vector quantities have, besides numerical value and unit, direction or orientation in space.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical%20quantity)
