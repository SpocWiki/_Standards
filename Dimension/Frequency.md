## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Frequency** is the number of occurrences of a repeating event per unit of time. 
> Frequency is an important parameter used in science and engineering 
> to specify the rate of oscillatory and vibratory phenomena, 
> such as mechanical vibrations, audio signals (sound), radio waves, and light.
>
> The interval of time between events is called the period. 
> It is the reciprocal of the frequency.  
> For example, if a heart beats at a frequency of 120 times per minute (2 hertz), 
> its period is one half of a second.
>
> Special definitions of frequency are used in certain contexts, 
> such as the angular frequency in rotational or cyclical properties, 
> when the rate of angular progress is measured. 
> Spatial frequency is defined for properties that vary or cccur repeatedly in geometry or space.
>
> The unit of measurement of frequency in the International System of Units (SI) is the hertz, 
> having the symbol Hz.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frequency)
