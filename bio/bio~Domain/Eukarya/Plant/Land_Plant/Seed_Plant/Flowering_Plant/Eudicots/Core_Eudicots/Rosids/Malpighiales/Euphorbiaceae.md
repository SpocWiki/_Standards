# [[Euphorbiaceae]] 

#is_/same_as :: [[../../../../../../../../../../../WikiData/WD~Euphorbiaceae,156584|WD~Euphorbiaceae,156584]] 

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Euphorbiaceae**, the spurge family, is a large family of flowering plants. 
> 
> In English, they are also commonly called euphorbias, 
> which is also the name of the type genus of the family. 
> 
> Most spurges, such as Euphorbia paralias, are herbs, 
> but some, especially in the tropics, are shrubs or trees, such as Hevea brasiliensis. 
> 
> Some, such as Euphorbia canariensis, are succulent and resemble cacti because of convergent evolution. 
> 
> This family has a cosmopolitan global distribution. 
> 
> The greatest diversity of species is in the tropics; however, 
> the Euphorbiaceae also have many species in nontropical areas of all continents except Antarctica.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euphorbiaceae)
