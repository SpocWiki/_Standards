#is_/same_as :: [[WD~Kumquat,5122903]] 

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Kumquat**s (KUM-kwot) (sometimes spelled cumquat in Australian and British English), 
> are a group of small, angiosperm, fruit-bearing trees in the family Rutaceae. 
> 
> Their taxonomy is disputed. 
> They were previously classified as forming the now-historical genus Fortunella 
> or placed within Citrus, sensu lato. 
> 
> Different classifications have alternatively assigned them to anywhere from a single species, Citrus japonica, to numerous species representing each cultivar. Recent genomic analysis defines three pure species, Citrus hindsii, C. margarita and C. crassifolia, with C. × japonica being a hybrid of the last two.
>
> The edible fruit closely resembles the orange (Citrus x sinensis) in color, texture, and anatomy, but is much smaller, being approximately the size of a large olive. The kumquat is a fairly cold-hardy citrus.
>
