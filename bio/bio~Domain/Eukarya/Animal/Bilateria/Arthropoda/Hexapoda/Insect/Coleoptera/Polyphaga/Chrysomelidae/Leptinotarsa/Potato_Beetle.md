## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> The Colorado potato beetle (Leptinotarsa decemlineata; also known as 
> the Colorado beetle, the ten-striped spearman, the ten-lined potato beetle, and the potato bug) 
> is a beetle known for being a major pest of potato crops. 
> 
> It is about 10 mm (3⁄8 in) long, with a bright yellow/orange body 
> and five bold brown stripes along the length of each of its wings. 
> 
> Native to the Rocky Mountains, it spread rapidly in potato crops across the United States 
> and then Europe from 1859 onwards.
>
> The Colorado potato beetle was first observed in 1811 by Thomas Nuttall 
> and was formally described in 1824 by American entomologist Thomas Say. 
> The beetles were collected in the Rocky Mountains, 
> where they were feeding on the buffalo bur, Solanum rostratum.
>
