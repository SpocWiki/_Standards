# Commelinanae

Containing group: [Monocotyledons](Monocotyledons.md)

### Introduction

The Commelinanae includes the grasses, sedges, bromeliads, and
relatives. This superorder includes approximately 25 families, 1075
genera, and 24000 species.

### Characteristics

The Commelinanae are characterized by scrobiculate pollen with
operculate apertures and orthotropous ovules, girdle-like endothecial
thickenings of the anthers, annulate pollen apertures, and apical
placentation (Linder and Kellogg, 1993).

### References

Linder, HP & EA Kellogg. 1995. Phylogenetic patterns in the commelinid
clade. pp 473-496 in: PJ Rudall, PJ Cribb, DF Cutler & CJ Humphries
(eds.). Monocotyledons: systematics and evolution. Royal Botanic Garden,
Kew.
