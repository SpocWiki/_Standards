#is_/same_as :: [[../../../../WikiData/WD~Surprise,333173|WD~Surprise,333173]]  

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Surprise** is a rapid, fleeting, mental and physiological state. 
> It is related to the startle response experienced by animals and humans 
> as the result of an unexpected event.
>
> Surprise can have any valence. That is, 
> it can be pleasant/positive, unpleasant/negative, or neutral/moderate. 
> 
> Surprise can occur in varying levels of intensity ranging from very surprised, 
> which may induce the fight-or-flight response, 
> or slightly surprised, which elicits a less intense response to the stimulus.
>
> Surprise is included as a primary or basic emotion in the taxonomies of Carroll Izard and Paul Ekman. According to these perspectives, surprise is evolutionarily adaptive, and also innate and universal across human cultures.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surprise%20(emotion))
