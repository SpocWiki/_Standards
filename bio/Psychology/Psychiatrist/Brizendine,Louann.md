#is_/part_of :: [[Butler-Bowdon~Psychology]] 

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Louann Brizendine** (born December 30, 1952) 
> is an American neuropsychiatrist who is both a researcher and a clinician 
> and professor at the [[UCSF(University of California, San Francisco)]]. 
> 
> She is the author of three books: 
> - The Female Brain (2006), 
> - The Male Brain (2010), and 
> - The Upgrade (2022).
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louann%20Brizendine)  

![[Brizendine,Louann.svg]]

## Louann Brizendine

Brizendine’s first degree was in neurobiology 
from the University of California,Berkeley (1972–6), 
followed by medicine at Yale University, 
and psychiatry at Harvard Medical School (1982–5).

After a stint teaching at Harvard, in 1988 she accepted a post 
at the Langley Porter Psychiatric Institute 
at the University of California, San Francisco, 
where in 1994 her Women’s Mood and Hormone Clinic was established. 

She continues to combine research work 
with clinical practice and teaching, 
focusing on the effects on mood, energy, sexual function, 
and hormonal influences on the brain.
