## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Kālidāsa** (Sanskrit: कालिदास, "Servant of Kali"; 4th–5th century CE) was a Classical Sanskrit author 
> who is often considered ancient India's greatest poet and playwright. 
> His plays and poetry are primarily based on Hindu Puranas and philosophy.  
> His surviving works consist of three plays, two epic poems and two shorter poems.
>
> Much about his life is unknown except what can be inferred from his poetry and plays. 
> His works cannot be dated with precision, 
> but they were most likely authored before the 5th century CE during the Gupta era. 
> Kalidas is mentioned as one of the seven Brahma avatars in Dasam Granth, written by Guru Gobind Singh.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalidasa)
