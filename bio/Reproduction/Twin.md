## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Twin**s are two offspring produced by the same pregnancy. 
> Twins can be either monozygotic ('identical'), meaning that they develop from one zygote, 
> which splits and forms two embryos, or dizygotic ('non-identical' or 'fraternal'), 
> meaning that each twin develops from a separate egg and each egg is fertilized by its own sperm cell. 
> 
> Since identical twins develop from one zygote, they will share the same sex, while fraternal twins may or may not. In very rare cases, fraternal or (semi-) identical twins can have the same mother and different fathers (heteropaternal superfecundation).
>
> In contrast, a fetus that develops alone in the womb (the much more common case in humans) is a singleton; one of a multiple birth is a multiple. Unrelated look-alikes whose resemblance parallels that of twins are referred to as doppelgänger.
>
