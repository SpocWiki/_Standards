## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Lacerta** is one of the 88 modern constellations defined by the International Astronomical Union. 
> Its name is Latin for lizard. A small, faint constellation, 
> it was defined in 1687 by the astronomer Johannes Hevelius. 
> 
> Its brightest stars form a "W" shape similar to that of Cassiopeia, 
> and it is thus sometimes referred to as 'Little Cassiopeia'. 
> 
> It is located between Cygnus, Cassiopeia and Andromeda on the northern celestial sphere. 
> The northern part lies on the Milky Way.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lacerta)
