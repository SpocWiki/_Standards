## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> Crater is a small constellation in the southern celestial hemisphere. 
> Its name is the Latinization of the Greek krater, a type of cup used to water down wine. 
> One of the 48 constellations listed by the second-century astronomer Ptolemy, 
> it depicts a cup that has been associated with the god Apollo 
> and is perched on the back of Hydra the water snake.
>
> There is no star brighter than third magnitude in the constellation. 
> Its two brightest stars, Delta Crateris of magnitude 3.56 
> and Alpha Crateris of magnitude 4.07, 
> are ageing orange giant stars that are cooler and larger than the Sun. 
> 
> Beta Crateris is a binary star system composed of a white giant star and a white dwarf. 
> 
> Seven star systems have been found to host planets. 
> A few notable galaxies, including Crater 2 and NGC 3981, 
> and a famous quasar lie within the borders of the constellation.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crater%20(constellation))
