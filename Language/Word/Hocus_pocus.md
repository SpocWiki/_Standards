# [[Hocus_pocus]] 

#is_/same_as :: [[WD~Hocus pocus,1624248]] 

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> Hocus-pocus is a reference to the actions of magicians, 
> often as the stereotypical magic words spoken when bringing about some sort of change. 
> 
> It was once a common term for a magician, juggler, or other similar entertainers. 
> In extended usage, the term is often used (pejoratively) 
> to describe irrational human activities that appear to depend on magic. 
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hocus-pocus)  

A theory is that it is a corruption or [parody](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parody "Parody") of the [Catholic liturgy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_liturgy "Catholic liturgy") of the [Eucharist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eucharist_\(Catholic_Church\) "Eucharist (Catholic Church)"), 
which contains the phrase "_[Hoc est enim corpus meum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Words_of_Institution "Words of Institution")_", meaning _This is my body_.
