## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> A **Verb** is a word that generally conveys an action (bring, read, walk, run, learn), 
> an occurrence (happen, become), or a state of being (be, exist, stand). 
> 
> In the usual description of English, the basic form, with or without the particle to, 
> is the infinitive. 
> 
> In many languages, verbs are inflected (modified in form) 
> to encode tense, aspect, mood, and voice. 
> 
> A verb may also agree with the person, gender or number of some of its arguments, 
> such as its subject, or object. 
> 
> In English, three tenses exist: 
> - present, to indicate that an action is being carried out; 
> - past, to indicate that an action has been done; and 
> - future, to indicate that an action will be done, expressed with the auxiliary verb will or shall.
>
> Every language discovered so far makes some form of noun-verb distinction, 
> possibly because of the **graph-like** nature of communicated meaning by humans, 
> i.e. nouns being the "entities" and verbs being the "links" between them. 
> 
> The word verb comes from Latin  verbum 'word or verb' 
> and shares the same Indo-European root as word.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Verb)
