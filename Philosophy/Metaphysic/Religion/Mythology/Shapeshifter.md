## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> In mythology, folklore and speculative fiction, shapeshifting is 
> the ability to physically transform oneself through unnatural means. 
> 
> The idea of shapeshifting is found in the oldest forms of totemism and shamanism, 
> as well as the oldest existent literature and epic poems such as 
> the Epic of Gilgamesh and the Iliad. 
> 
> The concept remains a common literary device in modern fantasy, 
> children's literature and popular culture. 
> 
> Examples of shape-shifters include changelings, jinns, kitsunes, vampires, 
> and werewolves, along with deities such as Loki and Vertumnus.
>
