## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> In Greek mythology, **Gaia** (; Ancient Greek: Γαῖα, romanized: Gaîa, a poetic form of Γῆ (Gê), 
> meaning 'land' or 'earth'), also spelled Gaea, is the personification of Earth. 
> 
> She is the mother of Uranus (Sky), with whom she conceived the Titans 
> (themselves parents of many of the Olympian gods), the Cyclopes, and the Giants, 
> as well as of Pontus (Sea), from whose union she bore the primordial sea gods. 
> Her equivalent in the Roman pantheon was [[../Roman_Mythology/Terra(mythology)|Terra]].
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaia)
