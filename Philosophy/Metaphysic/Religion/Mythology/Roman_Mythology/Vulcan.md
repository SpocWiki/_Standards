#is_/same_as :: [[../../../../../WikiData/WD~Vulcan,4640|WD~Vulcan,4640]]  

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Vulcan** (Latin: Vulcanus, in archaically retained spelling also Volcanus, 
> both pronounced [wʊɫˈkaːnʊs]) is the god of fire in ancient Roman religion and myth 
> including the fire of volcanoes, deserts, metalworking and the forge. 
> 
> He is often depicted with a blacksmith's hammer. 
> The Vulcanalia was the annual festival held August 23 in his honor. 
> His Greek counterpart is Hephaestus, the god of fire and smithery. 
> In Etruscan religion, he is identified with Sethlans.
>
> Vulcan belongs to the most ancient stage of Roman religion: 
> Varro, the ancient Roman scholar and writer, citing the Annales Maximi, 
> records that king Titus Tatius dedicated altars to a series of deities including Vulcan.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vulcan%20(mythology))
