## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Plutarch** (Ancient Greek: Πλούταρχος, Ploútarchos, Koine Greek: [ˈplúːtarkʰos]; c. AD 40 – 120s) was a Greek Middle Platonist philosopher, historian, biographer, essayist, 
> and priest at the Temple of Apollo in Delphi. 
> 
> He is known primarily for his Parallel Lives, a series of biographies 
> of illustrious Greeks and Romans, and Moralia, a collection of essays and speeches. 
> 
> Upon becoming a Roman citizen, he was possibly named Lucius Mestrius Plutarchus (Λούκιος Μέστριος Πλούταρχος).
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plutarch)
