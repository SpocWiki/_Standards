## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Kang Youwei** (Chinese: 康有為; Cantonese: Hōng Yáuh-wàih; 19 March 1858 – 31 March 1927) was a political thinker and reformer in China of the late Qing dynasty. 
> 
> His increasing closeness to and influence over the young Guangxu Emperor 
> sparked conflict between the emperor and his adoptive mother, the regent Empress Dowager Cixi.  
> His ideas were influential in the abortive Hundred Days' Reform. 
> Following the coup by Cixi that ended the reform, Kang was forced to flee. 
> He continued to advocate for a Chinese constitutional monarchy after the founding of the Republic of China.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kang%20Youwei)
