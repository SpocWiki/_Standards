## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> Max Ferdinand Scheler (German: [ˈʃeːlɐ]; 22 August 1874 – 19 May 1928) 
> was a German philosopher known for his work in phenomenology, ethics, 
> and philosophical anthropology. 
> 
> Considered in his lifetime one of the most prominent German philosophers, 
> Scheler developed the philosophical method of Edmund Husserl, 
> the founder of phenomenology.
>
> After Scheler's death in 1928, Martin Heidegger affirmed, with Ortega y Gasset, 
> that all philosophers of the century were indebted to Scheler and praised him as "the strongest philosophical force in modern Germany, nay, in contemporary Europe and in contemporary philosophy as such."
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Max%20Scheler)
