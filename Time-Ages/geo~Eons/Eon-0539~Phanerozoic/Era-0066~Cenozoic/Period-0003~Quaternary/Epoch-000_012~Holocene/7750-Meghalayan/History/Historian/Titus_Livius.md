## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> Titus Livius (Latin: [ˈtɪtʊs ˈliːwiʊs]; 59 BC – AD 17), 
> known in English as Livy (LIV-ee), was a Roman historian. 
> 
> He wrote a monumental history of Rome and the Roman people, 
> titled Ab Urbe Condita, ''From the Founding of the City'', 
> covering the period from the earliest legends of Rome 
> before the traditional founding in 753 BC 
> through the reign of Augustus in Livy's own lifetime. 
> 
> He was on good terms with members of the Julio-Claudian dynasty 
> and was a friend of Augustus. 
> 
> Livy encouraged Augustus’s young grandnephew, 
> the future emperor Claudius, to take up the writing of history.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Livy)
