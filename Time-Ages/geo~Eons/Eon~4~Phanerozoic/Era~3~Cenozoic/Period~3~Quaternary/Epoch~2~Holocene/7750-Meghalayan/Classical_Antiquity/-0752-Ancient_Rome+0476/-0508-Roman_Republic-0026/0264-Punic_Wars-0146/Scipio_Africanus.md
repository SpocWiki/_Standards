#is_/same_as :: [[WD~Scipio_Africanus,2253]] 

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> Publius Cornelius Scipio Africanus (Latin: [ˈskiːpioː]; 236/235–c. 183 BC) 
> was a Roman general and statesman who was one of the main architects of 
> Rome's victory against Carthage in the Second Punic War. 
> 
> Often regarded as one of the greatest military commanders and strategists of all time, 
> his greatest military achievement was the defeat of Hannibal at the Battle of Zama in 202 BC. 
> This victory in Africa earned him the honorific epithet Africanus, 
> literally meaning 'the African', but meant to be understood as a conqueror of Africa.
>
> Scipio's conquest of Carthaginian Iberia culminated in the Battle of Ilipa in 206 BC 
> against Hannibal's brother Mago Barca. 
> 
> Although considered a hero by the Roman people, 
> primarily for his victories against Carthage, 
> Scipio had many opponents, especially Cato the Elder, who hated him deeply. 
> 
> In 187 BC, he was tried in a show trial alongside his brother 
> for bribes they supposedly received from the Seleucid king Antiochus III 
> during the Roman–Seleucid War. 
> 
> Disillusioned by the ingratitude of his peers, 
> Scipio left Rome and retired from public life at his villa in Liternum.
>
