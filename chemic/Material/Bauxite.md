# [[Bauxite]] 

#is_/same_as :: [[../../WikiData/WD~Bauxite,102078|WD~Bauxite,102078]] 

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Bauxite** is a sedimentary rock with a relatively high aluminium content. 
> It is the world's main source of aluminium and gallium. 
> 
> Bauxite consists mostly of the aluminium minerals gibbsite (Al(OH)3), 
> boehmite (γ-AlO(OH)), and diaspore (α-AlO(OH)), 
> mixed with the two iron oxides goethite (FeO(OH)) and haematite (Fe2O3), 
> the aluminium clay mineral kaolinite (Al2Si2O5(OH)4) 
> and small amounts of anatase (TiO2) and ilmenite (FeTiO3 or FeO·TiO2).
>
> Bauxite appears dull in luster and is reddish-brown, white, or tan.
>
> In 1821, the French geologist Pierre Berthier discovered bauxite 
> near the village of Les Baux in Provence, southern France.
>
