## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **James Prescott Joule** (24 December 1818 –  11 October 1889) was an English physicist. 
> Joule studied the nature of heat and discovered its relationship to mechanical work. 
> This led to the law of conservation of energy, 
> which in turn led to the development of the first law of thermodynamics. 
> The SI unit of energy, the joule (J), is named after him.
>
> He worked with Lord Kelvin to develop an absolute thermodynamic temperature scale, 
> which came to be called the Kelvin scale. 
> 
> Joule also made observations of magnetostriction, 
> and he found the relationship between the current through a resistor and the heat dissipated, 
> which is also called Joule's first law. 
> 
> His experiments about energy transformations were first published in 1843.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James%20Prescott%20Joule)
