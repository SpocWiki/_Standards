## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> Santiago Ramón y Cajal (Spanish: [sanˈtjaɣo raˈmon i kaˈxal]; 1 May 1852 – 17 October 1934) 
> was a Spanish neuroscientist, pathologist, and histologist 
> specializing in neuroanatomy, and the central nervous system. 
> 
> He and Camillo Golgi received the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1906. 
> Ramón y Cajal was the first Spaniard to win a scientific Nobel Prize. 
> His original investigations of the microscopic structure of the brain 
> made him a pioneer of modern neuroscience.
>
> Hundreds of his drawings illustrating the arborization (tree-like growth) of brain cells are still in use, since the mid-20th century, for educational and training purposes.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santiago%20Ram%C3%B3n%20y%20Cajal)
