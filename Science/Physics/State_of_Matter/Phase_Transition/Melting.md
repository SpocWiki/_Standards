# [[Melting]] 

#is_/same_as :: [[WD~Melting,106080]]

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Melting**, or fusion, is a physical process that results in the phase transition of a substance 
> from a solid to a liquid. 
> 
> This occurs when the internal energy of the solid increases, 
> typically by the application of heat or pressure, 
> which increases the substance's temperature to the melting point. 
> 
> At the melting point, the ordering of ions or molecules in the solid breaks down 
> to a less ordered state, and the solid melts to become a liquid.
>
> Substances in the molten state generally have 
> viscosity reduced as the temperature increases. 
> 
> An exception to this principle is elemental sulfur, 
> whose viscosity increases in the range of 130 °C to 190 °C due to polymerization.
>
> Some organic compounds melt through mesophases, 
> states of partial order between solid and liquid.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melting)
