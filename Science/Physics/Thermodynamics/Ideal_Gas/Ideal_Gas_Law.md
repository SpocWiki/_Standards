# [[Ideal_Gas_Law]] 

#is_/same_as :: [[WD~Ideal_gas_law,191785]] 

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> The ideal gas law, also called the general gas equation, 
> is the equation of state of a hypothetical ideal gas. 
> 
> It is a good approximation of the behavior of many gases under many conditions, 
> although it has several limitations with high Densities(Pressures) or low Temperatures or Volumes. 
> 
> It was first stated by Benoît Paul Émile Clapeyron in 1834 as a combination of the empirical 
> - Boyle's law, Charles's law, Avogadro's law, and Gay-Lussac's law. 
> 
> The ideal gas law is often written in an empirical form: pV=nRT with
> - p the pressure, 
> - V volume 
> - T temperature 
> - n amount (i.e. number) of substance n; and
> - R the ideal gas constant.
>
> It can also be derived from the microscopic kinetic theory, 
> as was achieved (independently) by August Krönig in 1856 and Rudolf Clausius in 1857.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ideal%20gas%20law)
