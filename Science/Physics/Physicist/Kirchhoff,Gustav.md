## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> Gustav Robert Kirchhoff (German: [ˈgʊstaːf ˈʁoːbɛʁt ˈkɪʁçhɔf]; 12 March 1824 – 17 October 1887) 
> was a German chemist, mathematician, physicist, and spectroscopist 
> who contributed to the fundamental understanding of electrical circuits, 
> spectroscopy and the emission of black-body radiation by heated objects. 
> He also coined the term black body in 1860.
>
> Several different sets of concepts are named "Kirchhoff's laws" after him, 
> which include Kirchhoff's circuit laws, Kirchhoff's law of thermal radiation, 
> and Kirchhoff's law of thermochemistry.
>
> The Bunsen–Kirchhoff Award for spectroscopy is named after Kirchhoff 
> and his colleague, Robert Bunsen.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gustav%20Kirchhoff)
