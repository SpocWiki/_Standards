## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> Philipp Eduard Anton von Lenard (German: [ˈfɪlɪp ˈleːnaʁt] ; 7 June 1862 – 20 May 1947) 
> was a Hungarian–German experimental physicist 
> who received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1905 for his work on cathode rays. 
> 
> This work led to his experimental realization of the photoelectric effect, 
> discovering that the energy (speed) of the electrons ejected from a cathode 
> depends only on the frequency, and not the intensity of light.
>
> As an active proponent of the Nazi ideology, Lenard supported Adolf Hitler in the 1920s 
> and was an important role model for the Deutsche Physik movement during the Nazi period. 
> 
> Notably, he labeled Albert Einstein's contributions to theoretical physics as "Jewish physics".
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philipp%20Lenard)
