## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> Arnold Johannes Wilhelm Sommerfeld (German: [ˈaʁnɔlt joˈhanəs ˈvɪlhɛlm ˈzɔmɐˌfɛlt]; 
> 5 December 1868 – 26 April 1951) was a German theoretical physicist 
> who pioneered developments in atomic and quantum physics, 
> and also educated and mentored many students for the new era of theoretical physics. 
> 
> He served as doctoral advisor and postdoc advisor to seven Nobel Prize winners 
> and supervised at least 30 other famous physicists and chemists. 
> Only J. J. Thomson's record of mentorship offers a comparable list of high-achieving students.
>
> He introduced the second quantum number, azimuthal quantum number, 
> and the third quantum number, magnetic quantum number. 
> He also introduced the fine-structure constant and pioneered X-ray wave theory.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arnold%20Sommerfeld)
