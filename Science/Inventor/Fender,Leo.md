> Clarence Leonidas Fender (August 10, 1909 â€“ March 21, 1991) 
> was an American inventor and founder of the Fender Musical Instruments Corporation. 
> 
> Fender designed the company's iconic early instruments: 
> - the Fender Telecaster, the first mass-produced solid-body electric guitar; 
> - the Fender Stratocaster, among the most iconic electric guitars; and 
> - the Fender Precision Bass, which set the standard for electric basses. 
> 
> He also designed the Fender Bassman amplifier, 
> which became the archetype for later amplifiers (notably by Marshall and Mesa Boogie) 
> that dominated rock and roll music.
>
> Fender, who was not a guitarist himself, 
> was inducted posthumously into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1992.
>
