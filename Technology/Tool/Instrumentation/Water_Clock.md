# [[Water_Clock]] 

#is_/same_as :: [[WD~Water_clock,194173]] 

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> A water clock, or clepsydra (from Ancient Greek κλεψύδρα (klepsúdra) 'pipette, water clock'; from  κλέπτω (kléptō) 'to steal' and  ὕδωρ (hydor) 'water'; lit. ' water thief'), 
> is a timepiece by which time is measured by the regulated flow of liquid 
> into (inflow type) or out from (outflow type) a vessel, 
> and where the amount of liquid can then be measured.
>
> Water clocks are some of the oldest time-measuring instruments. 
> The simplest form of water clock, with a bowl-shaped outflow, 
> existed in Babylon, Egypt, and Persia around the 16th century BC. 
> 
> Other regions of the world, including India and China, 
> also provide early evidence of water clocks, but the earliest dates are less certain. 
> 
> Water clocks were used in ancient Greece and in ancient Rome, 
> as described by technical writers such as Ctesibius (died 222 BC) 
> and Vitruvius (died after 15 BC).
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water%20clock)
