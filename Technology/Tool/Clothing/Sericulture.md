## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Sericulture**, or silk farming, is the cultivation of silkworms to produce silk. 
> 
> Although there are several commercial species of silkworms, 
> the caterpillar of the domestic silkmoth is the most widely used 
> and intensively studied silkworm. 
> 
> This species of silkmoth is no longer found in the wild 
> as they have been modified through selective breeding, 
> rendering most flightless and without defense against predators. 
> 
> Silk is believed to have first been produced in China as early as the Neolithic period.   
> Today, China and India are the two main producers, 
> with more than 60% of the world's annual production.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sericulture)
