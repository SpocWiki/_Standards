## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> A **Wheel** is a rotating component (typically circular in shape) that is intended to turn on an axle bearing. 
> The wheel is one of the key components of the wheel and axle which is one of the six simple machines. 
> Wheels, in conjunction with axles, allow heavy objects to be moved easily 
> facilitating movement or transportation while supporting a load, or performing labor in machines. 
> Wheels are also used for other purposes, such as a ship's wheel, steering wheel, potter's wheel, and flywheel.
>
> Common examples can be found in transport applications. 
> A wheel reduces friction by facilitating motion by rolling together with the use of axles. 
> In order for wheels to rotate, a moment needs to be applied to the wheel about its axis, 
> either by way of gravity or by the application of another external force or torque.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wheel) 

Potter's wheels were in common use long before the Wheel was applied to carts. 
New Research indicates that the wheel was used for transport first around the Black Sea ca. 5000BC.
