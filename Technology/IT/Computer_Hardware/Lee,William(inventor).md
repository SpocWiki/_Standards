## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> William Lee (1563–1614) was an English inventor and alleged clergyman 
> who invented the stocking frame knitting machine. 
> 
> Framework knitting, as the use of Lee's machine in stocking production was called, 
> was the first major stage in the mechanisation of the textile industry, 
> a process that 200 years later precipitated the Industrial Revolution.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William%20Lee%20(inventor))  

He was denied Patent twice, 1589 and 1598 by Queen Elizabeth, 
so he moved to France eventually, where he established Business in Rouen.

On Resolution of the Edict of Nantes in 1685, many Hugenottes 
fled to German States like Prussia, Brandenburg and Saxony.
