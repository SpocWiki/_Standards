#is_/same_as :: [[../../../../../WikiData/WD~Lever,40164|WD~Lever,40164]] 

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> A **Lever** is a simple machine consisting of a beam or rigid rod 
> pivoted at a fixed hinge, or fulcrum. 
> 
> A lever is a rigid body capable of rotating on a point on itself. 
> On the basis of the locations of fulcrum, load, and effort, the lever is divided into three types. 
> It is one of the six simple machines identified by Renaissance scientists. 
> A lever amplifies an input force to provide a greater output force, 
> which is said to provide leverage, which is mechanical advantage gained in the system, 
> equal to the ratio of the output force to the input force. 
> As such, the lever is a mechanical advantage device, trading off force against movement.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lever)
