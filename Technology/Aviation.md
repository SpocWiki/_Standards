## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Aviation** includes the activities surrounding mechanical flight and the aircraft industry. 
> Aircraft includes fixed-wing and rotary-wing types, morphable wings, wing-less lifting bodies, 
> as well as lighter-than-air aircraft such as hot air balloons and airships.
>
> Aviation began in the 18th century with the development of the hot air balloon, 
> an apparatus capable of atmospheric displacement through buoyancy. 
> 
> Clément Ader built the "Ader Éole" in France and made an uncontrolled, powered hop in 1890. 
> This is the first powered aircraft, although it did not achieve controlled flight. 
> Some of the most significant advancements in aviation technology came with the controlled gliding flying of Otto Lilienthal in 1896; then a large step in significance came with the construction of the first powered airplane by the Wright brothers in the early 1900s. Since that time, aviation has been technologically revolutionized by the introduction of the jet which permitted a major form of transport throughout the world.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aviation)
