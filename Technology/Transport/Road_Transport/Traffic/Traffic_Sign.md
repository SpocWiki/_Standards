## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> Traffic signs or road signs are signs erected at the side of or above roads 
> to give instructions or provide information to road users. 
> 
> The earliest signs were simple wooden or stone milestones. 
> Later, signs with directional arms were introduced, for example 
> the fingerposts in the United Kingdom 
> and their wooden counterparts in Saxony.
>
> With traffic volumes increasing since the 1930s, 
> many countries have adopted pictorial signs or 
> otherwise simplified and standardized their signs to overcome language barriers, 
> and enhance traffic safety. 
> 
> Such pictorial signs use symbols (often silhouettes) in place of words 
> and are usually based on international protocols. 
> 
> Such signs were first developed in Europe, 
> and have been adopted by most countries to varying degrees.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traffic%20sign)
