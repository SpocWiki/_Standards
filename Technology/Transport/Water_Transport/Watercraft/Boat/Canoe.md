## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> A **Canoe** is a lightweight, narrow water vessel, typically pointed at both ends and open on top, 
> propelled by one or more seated or kneeling paddlers facing the direction of travel and using paddles.
>
> In British English, the term canoe can also refer to a kayak, 
> whereas canoes are then called Canadian or open canoes to distinguish them from kayaks. 
> 
> However, for official competition purposes, 
> the American distinction between a kayak and a canoe is almost always adopted. 
> 
> At the Olympics, both conventions are used: 
> under the umbrella terms Canoe Slalom and Canoe Sprint, 
> there are separate events for canoes and kayaks.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canoe)
