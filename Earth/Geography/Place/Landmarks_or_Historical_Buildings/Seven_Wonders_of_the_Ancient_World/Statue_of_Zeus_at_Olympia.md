# [[Statue_of_Zeus_at_Olympia]] 

#is_/same_as :: [[../../../../../WikiData/WD~Statue_of_Zeus_at_Olympia,46239|WD~Statue_of_Zeus_at_Olympia,46239]]  

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> The Statue of Zeus at Olympia was a giant seated figure, about 12.4 m (41 ft) tall, 
> made by the Greek sculptor Phidias around 435 BC 
> at the sanctuary of Olympia, Greece, and erected in the Temple of Zeus there.  
> 
> Zeus is the sky and thunder god in ancient Greek religion, 
> who rules as king of the gods on Mount Olympus.
>
> The statue was a chryselephantine sculpture of 
> ivory plates and gold panels on a wooden framework. 
> 
> Zeus sat on a painted cedarwood throne 
> ornamented with ebony, ivory, gold, and precious stones. 
> It was one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. 
>
> The statue was lost and destroyed before the end of the 6th century AD, 
> with conflicting accounts of the date and circumstances. 
> 
> Details of its form are known only from ancient Greek descriptions 
> and representations on coins and art.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statue%20of%20Zeus%20at%20Olympia)
