#is_/same_as :: [[WD~Tropic_of_Capricorn,177440]] 

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> The Tropic of Capricorn (or the Southern Tropic) 
> is the circle of latitude that contains the subsolar point at the December (or southern) solstice. 
> 
> It is thus the southernmost latitude where the Sun can be seen directly overhead. 
> It also reaches 90 degrees below the horizon at solar midnight on the June Solstice. 
> Its northern equivalent is the Tropic of Cancer.
>
> The Tropic of Capricorn is one of the five major circles of latitude marked on maps of Earth. 
> Its latitude is currently 23°26′09.3″ (or 23.43592°) south of the Equator, 
> but it is very gradually moving northward, currently at the rate of 0.47 arcseconds, or 15 metres, per year.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropic%20of%20Capricorn)
