#is_/same_as :: [[WD~Red_Sea,23406]] 

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> The Red Sea is a sea inlet of the Indian Ocean, lying between Africa and Asia. 
> Its connection to the ocean is in the south, 
> through the Bab-el-Mandeb Strait and the Gulf of Aden. 
> To its north lies the Sinai Peninsula, the Gulf of Aqaba, and the Gulf of Suez, 
> which leads into to the Suez Canal. 
> 
> It is underlain by the Red Sea Rift, which is part of the Great Rift Valley.
>
> The Red Sea has a surface area of roughly 438,000 km2 (169,000 sq mi), 
> is about 2,250 km (1,400 mi) long, and 355 km (221 mi) wide at its widest point. 
> It has an average depth of 490 m (1,610 ft), and in the central Suakin Trough, 
> it reaches its maximum depth of 3,040 m (9,970 ft).
>
> The Red Sea is quite shallow, with approximately 40% at less than 100 m (330 ft) deep, 
> and approximately 25% at less than 50 m (160 ft) deep. 
> 
> The extensive shallow shelves are noted for their marine life and corals. 
> More than 1,000 invertebrate species and 200 types of soft and hard coral live in the sea. 
> The Red Sea is the world's northernmost tropical sea and has been designated a Global 200 ecoregion.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red%20Sea)
