## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> Vladimir Ivanovich Vernadsky (Russian: Владимир Иванович Вернадский), 
> also spelt Volodymyr Ivanovych Vernadsky (Ukrainian: Володимир Іванович Вернадський; 12 March [O.S. 28 February] 1863 – 6 January 1945), 
> was a Russian, Ukrainian, and Soviet mineralogist and geochemist 
> who is considered one of the founders of geochemistry, biogeochemistry, and radiogeology. 
> 
> He was one of the founders and the first president of the 
> Ukrainian Academy of Sciences (now National Academy of Sciences of Ukraine). 
> 
> Vladimir Vernadsky is most noted for his 1926 book The Biosphere 
> in which he inadvertently worked to popularize Eduard Suess's 1875 term biosphere, 
> by hypothesizing that life is the geological force that shapes the earth. 
> 
> In 1943 he was awarded the Stalin Prize. 
> Vernadsky's portrait is depicted on the Ukrainian ₴1,000 hryvnia banknote.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vladimir%20Vernadsky)
