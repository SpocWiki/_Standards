## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> The **Congo River**, formerly also known as the Zaire River, 
> is the second-longest river in Africa, shorter only than the Nile, 
> as well as the third-largest river in the world by discharge volume, 
> following the Amazon and Ganges rivers. 
> 
> It is the world's deepest recorded river, with measured depths of around 220 m (720 ft). 
> The Congo–Lualaba–Luvua–Luapula–Chambeshi River system has an overall length of 4,700 km, 
> which makes it the world's ninth-longest river. 
> 
> The Chambeshi is a tributary of the Lualaba River, 
> and Lualaba is the name of the Congo River upstream of Boyoma Falls, 
> extending for 1,800 km (1,100 mi).
>
> Measured along with the Lualaba, the Congo River has a total length of 4,370 km. 
> It is the only major river to cross the equator twice. 
> The Congo Basin has a total area of about 4,000,000 km2 (1,500,000 sq mi), 
> or 13% of the entire African landmass.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congo%20River)
