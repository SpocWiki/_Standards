## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> In physics and chemistry, binding energy is the smallest amount of energy required 
> to remove a particle from a system of particles 
> or to disassemble a system of particles into individual parts. 
> 
> In the former meaning the term is 
> predominantly used in condensed matter physics, atomic physics, and chemistry, 
> whereas in nuclear physics the term separation energy is used. 
> 
> A bound system is typically at a lower energy level than its unbound constituents. 
> According to relativity theory, a ΔE decrease in the total energy of a system 
> is accompanied by a decrease Δm in the total mass, where Δmc2 = ΔE.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Binding%20energy)
