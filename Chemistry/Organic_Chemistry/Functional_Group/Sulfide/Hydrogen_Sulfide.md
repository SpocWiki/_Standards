## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Hydrogen sulfide** (IUPAC name and American English) or hydrogen sulphide (Commonwealth) 
> is a chemical compound with the formula H2S. 
> 
> It is a colorless hydrogen chalcogenide gas, and is toxic, corrosive, and flammable. 
> 
> Trace amounts in ambient atmosphere have a characteristic foul odor of rotten eggs.  
> 
> Swedish chemist Carl Wilhelm Scheele is credited with having 
> discovered the chemical composition of purified hydrogen sulfide in 1777.
>
> Hydrogen sulfide is toxic to humans and most other animals 
> by inhibiting cellular respiration in a manner similar to [[../Cyanide/Hydrogen_Cyanide]]. 
> 
> When it is inhaled or its salts are ingested in high amounts, 
> damage to organs occurs rapidly with symptoms ranging from 
> breathing difficulties to convulsions and death. 
> 
> Despite this, the human body produces small amounts of this sulfide and its mineral salts, 
> and uses it as a signalling molecule.
>
> Hydrogen sulfide is often produced from the 
> microbial breakdown of organic matter in the absence of oxygen, 
> such as in swamps and sewers; this process is commonly known as anaerobic digestion, 
> which is done by sulfate-reducing microorganisms. 
> 
> It also occurs in volcanic gases, natural gas deposits, and sometimes in well-drawn water.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen%20sulfide)
