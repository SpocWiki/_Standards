## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Fermium** is a synthetic chemical element; it has symbol Fm and atomic number 100. 
> It is an actinide and the heaviest element 
> that can be formed by neutron bombardment of lighter elements, 
> and hence the last element that can be prepared in macroscopic quantities, 
> although pure fermium metal has not been  prepared yet. 
> A total of 20 isotopes are known, with 257Fm being the longest-lived with a half-life of 100.5 days.
>
> Fermium was discovered in the debris of the first hydrogen bomb explosion in 1952, 
> and named after Enrico Fermi, one of the pioneers of nuclear physics. 
> Its chemistry is typical for the late actinides, with a preponderance of the +3 oxidation state 
> but also an accessible +2 oxidation state. 
> 
> Owing to the small amounts of produced fermium 
> and all of its isotopes having relatively short half-lives, 
> there are currently no uses for it outside basic scientific research.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fermium)
