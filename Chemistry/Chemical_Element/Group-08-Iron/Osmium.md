## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Osmium** (from Ancient Greek  ὀσμή (osmḗ) 'smell') 
> is a chemical element with the symbol has_name_abbreviated = `=this.dv_has_name_abbreviated`  and atomic number has_number_of_electrons = `=this.dv_has_number_of_electrons` . 
> It is a hard, brittle, bluish-white transition metal in the platinum group 
> that is found as a trace element in alloys, mostly in platinum ores. 
> 
> Osmium is the densest naturally occurring element. 
> When experimentally measured using X-ray crystallography, 
> it has a density of has_value_for_density = `=this.dv_has_value_for_density`  g/cm³. 
> 
> Manufacturers use its alloys with platinum, iridium, and other platinum-group metals 
> to make fountain pen nib tipping, electrical contacts, 
> and in other applications that require extreme durability and hardness.
> 
> Osmium is among the rarest elements in the Earth's crust, making up only 50 parts per trillion (ppt). 
> It is estimated to be about 0.6 parts per billion in the universe 
> and is therefore the rarest precious metal.
>
