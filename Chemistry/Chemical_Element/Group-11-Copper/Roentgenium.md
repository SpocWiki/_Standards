# [[Roentgenium]] 

#is_/same_as :: [[../../../WikiData/WD~Roentgenium,1272|WD~Roentgenium,1272]]  

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Roentgenium** (German: [ʁœntˈɡeːni̯ʊm] ) is a synthetic chemical element; 
> it has symbol Rg and atomic number 111. 
> 
> It is extremely radioactive and can only be created in a laboratory. 
> The most stable known isotope, roentgenium-282, has a half-life of 130 seconds, 
> although the unconfirmed roentgenium-286 
> may have a longer half-life of about 10.7 minutes. 
> 
> Roentgenium was first created in December 1994 
> by the GSI Helmholtz Centre for Heavy Ion Research near Darmstadt, Germany. 
> 
> It is named after the physicist Wilhelm Röntgen (also spelled Roentgen), 
> who discovered X-rays. 
> Only a few roentgenium atoms have ever been synthesized, and they have no practical application.
>
> In the periodic table, it is a d-block transactinide element. 
> It is a member of the 7th period and is placed in the group 11 elements, 
> although no chemical experiments have been carried out 
> to confirm that it behaves as the heavier homologue to gold in group 11 
> as the ninth member of the 6d series of transition metals. 
> 
> Roentgenium is calculated to have similar properties to its lighter homologues, 
> copper, silver, and gold, although it may show some differences from them.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roentgenium)
