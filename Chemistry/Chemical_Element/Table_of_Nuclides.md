## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> A table or chart of nuclides is a two-dimensional graph of isotopes of the chemical elements, 
> in which one axis represents the number of neutrons (symbol N) 
> and the other represents the number of protons (atomic number, symbol Z) in the atomic nucleus.  
> 
> Each point plotted on the graph thus represents a nuclide of a known or hypothetical element. 
> 
> This system of ordering nuclides can offer a greater insight into the characteristics of isotopes 
> than the better-known [[Periodic_Table|Periodic_Table]], which shows only elements and **not their isotopes**.  
> 
> The chart of the nuclides is also known as the Segrè chart, after Italian physicist Emilio Segrè.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Table%20of%20nuclides)
