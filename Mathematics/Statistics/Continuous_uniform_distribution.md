## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> In probability theory and statistics, the continuous uniform distributions or rectangular distributions are a family of symmetric probability distributions. Such a distribution describes an experiment where there is an arbitrary outcome that lies between certain bounds. The bounds are defined by the parameters, 
> a and b which are the minimum and maximum values. The interval can 
> either be closed (i.e. [a,b]) or open (i.e. (a,b)). 
> Therefore, the distribution is often abbreviated U(a,b),
>  where 
>  stands for uniform distribution. 
>  
>  The difference between the bounds defines the interval length; 
>  all intervals of the same length on the distribution's support are equally probable. 
>  It is the maximum entropy probability distribution for a random variable X 
>  under no constraint other than that it is contained in the distribution's support.
>
