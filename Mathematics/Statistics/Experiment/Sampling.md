## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> In signal processing, **Sampling** is 
> the reduction of a continuous-time signal to a discrete-time signal. 
> A common example is the conversion of a sound wave to a sequence of "samples".
>
> A sample is a value of the signal at a point in time and/or space; 
> this definition differs from the term's usage in statistics, which refers to a set of such values.
>
> A sampler is a subsystem or operation that extracts samples from a continuous signal. 
> A theoretical ideal sampler produces samples equivalent to the instantaneous value of the continuous signal at the desired points.
>
> The original signal can be reconstructed from a sequence of samples, up to the Nyquist limit, by passing the sequence of samples through a reconstruction filter.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sampling%20(signal%20processing))
