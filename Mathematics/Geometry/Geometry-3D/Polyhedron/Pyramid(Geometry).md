## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> A pyramid is a polyhedron (a geometric figure) formed 
> by connecting a polygonal base and a point, called the apex. 
> 
> Each base edge and apex form a triangle, called a lateral face. 
> A pyramid is a conic solid with a polygonal base. 
> 
> **All pyramids are self-dual**.
> 
> Many types of pyramids can be found by determining the shape of bases, 
> either by based on a regular polygon (regular pyramids) 
> or by cutting off the apex (truncated pyramid). 
> 
> It can be generalized into higher dimensions, known as hyperpyramid. 
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyramid%20(geometry))
