## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

[[Arnold,Vladimir]] himself wrote in 1998 satirically:

> Similarly to the fact that [America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Americas "Americas") does not carry [Columbus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christopher_Columbus "Christopher Columbus")'s name, 
> mathematical results are almost never called by the names of their discoverers. [â€¦..] 
> Prof. [M. Berry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Berry_\(physicist\) "Michael Berry (physicist)") once formulated the following two principles:
> 
> - _The Arnold principle_. If a notion bears a personal name, then this name is not the name of the discoverer.
> - _The Berry principle_. The Arnold Principle is applicable to itself.
