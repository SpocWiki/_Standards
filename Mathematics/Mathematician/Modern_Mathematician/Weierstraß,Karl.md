## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> Karl Theodor Wilhelm Weierstrass (German: Weierstraß [ˈvaɪɐʃtʁaːs]; 31 October 1815 – 19 February 1897) 
> was a German mathematician often cited as the "father of modern analysis". 
> 
> Despite leaving university without a degree, 
> he studied mathematics and trained as a school teacher, 
> eventually teaching mathematics, physics, botany and gymnastics. 
> He later received an honorary doctorate and became professor of mathematics in Berlin.
>
> Among many other contributions, 
> Weierstrass  formalized the definition of the continuity of a function and complex analysis, 
> proved the intermediate value theorem and the Bolzano–Weierstrass theorem, 
> and used the latter to study the properties of continuous functions on closed bounded intervals.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karl%20Weierstrass)
