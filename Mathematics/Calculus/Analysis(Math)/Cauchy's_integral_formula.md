## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> In mathematics, Cauchy's integral formula, named after Augustin-Louis Cauchy, 
> is a central statement in complex analysis. 
> 
> It expresses the fact that a holomorphic function defined on a disk 
> is completely determined by its values on the boundary of the disk, 
> and it provides integral formulas for all derivatives of a holomorphic function. 
> 
> Cauchy's formula shows that, in complex analysis, "differentiation is equivalent to integration": 
> complex differentiation, like integration, behaves well under uniform limits – 
> a result that does not hold in real analysis.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cauchy's%20integral%20formula)
