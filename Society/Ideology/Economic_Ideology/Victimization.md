## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> Victimisation (or **Victimization**) is the state or 
> process of being victimised or becoming a victim, i.e. 
> being [harmed](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/harm#English "harm"), [killed](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/kill#English "kill"), [injured](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/injure#English "injure"), [subjected](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/subject#English "subject") to [oppression](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/oppression#English "oppression"), [deceit](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/deceit#English "deceit"), or otherwise [adversely](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/adversely#English "adversely") [affected](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/affect#English "affect")—by [someone](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/someone#English "someone") or [something](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/something#English "something"), especially [another](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/another#English "another") person or event, force, or condition. 
> 
> The field that studies the process, rates, incidence, effects, and prevalence of victimisation is called victimology.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Victimisation)
