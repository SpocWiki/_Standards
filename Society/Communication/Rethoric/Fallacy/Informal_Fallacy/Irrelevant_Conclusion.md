# [[Irrelevant_Conclusion]] 

#is_/same_as :: [[_Standards/WikiData/WD~irrelevant_conclusion,737677]]

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> An **Irrelevant conclusion**, also known as ignoratio elenchi (Latin for 'ignoring refutation') 
> or missing the point, is the informal fallacy of 
> presenting an argument whose conclusion fails to address the issue in question. 
> It falls into the broad class of relevance fallacies.
> 
> The irrelevant conclusion should not be confused with formal fallacy, 
> an argument whose conclusion does not follow from its premises; instead, it is that 
> **despite its formal consistency it is not relevant to the subject** being talked about.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irrelevant%20conclusion)
