## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Around the World in Eighty Days** (French: Le Tour du monde en quatre-vingts jours) 
> is an adventure novel by the French writer [[../Verne,Jules|Verne,Jules]], first published in French in 1872. 
> 
> In the story, [[Fogg,Phileas|Phileas Fogg]] of London and his newly employed French valet Passepartout 
> attempt to circumnavigate the world in 80 days on a wager of £20,000 
> (equivalent to £2.3 million in 2023) set by his friends at the Reform Club. 
> It is one of Verne's most acclaimed works.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Around%20the%20World%20in%20Eighty%20Days) 


## Other World-Roundtrips 

### Concorde;33 Hours 

On 12–13 October 1992, 
in commemoration of the 500th anniversary of [Columbus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christopher_Columbus "Christopher Columbus")' first voyage to the [New World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_World "New World"), 
Concorde Spirit Tours (US) chartered Air France Concorde F-BTSD 
and [circumnavigated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circumnavigation "Circumnavigation") the world in 32 hours 49 minutes and 3 seconds, 
from [Lisbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lisbon "Lisbon"), Portugal, including six refueling stops at 
- [Santo Domingo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santo_Domingo "Santo Domingo"), 
- [Acapulco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acapulco "Acapulco"), 
- [Honolulu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Honolulu "Honolulu"), 
- Guam, 
- [Bangkok](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangkok "Bangkok"), and 
- [Bahrain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bahrain "Bahrain"). 


### ISS; 92 Minutes 

The International Space Station completes one orbit around Earth in approximately:
- **92 minutes**
- ≈ **1.53 hours**
- ≈ **15.5 orbits per day**

The station travels at roughly:
- **27 600 km/h**
- ≈ **7.66 km/s**
- ≈ **17 150 mph**

Its orbit is at an altitude of about **400 km** above Earth’s surface, 
though this varies slightly due to atmospheric drag and periodic reboost maneuvers.

A simplified orbital-period estimate comes from Keplerian mechanics:
$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{a^3}{µ}}$$


| Symbol | Meaning                            |                      Typical ISS value |
| ------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------: |
| (T)    | Orbital period                     |                                 92 min |
| (a)    | Orbital radius from Earth’s center |                              ≈ 6770 km |
| µ  | Earth gravitational parameter      | (3.986\times10^{14}\ \mathrm{m^3/s^2}) |

Because Earth rotates underneath the ISS, it does **not** pass over the exact same ground track every orbit.
