## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> John Griffith London (né Chaney; January 12, 1876 – November 22, 1916), 
> better known as Jack London, was an American novelist, journalist and activist. 
> 
> A pioneer of commercial fiction and American magazines, 
> he was one of the first American authors to become an international celebrity 
> and **earn a large fortune from writing**. 
> He was also an innovator in the genre that would later become known as science fiction.
>
> London was part of the radical literary group "The Crowd" in San Francisco 
> and a passionate advocate of animal welfare, workers' rights and socialism. 
> London wrote several works dealing with these topics, such as 
> - his dystopian novel The Iron Heel, 
> - his non-fiction exposé The People of the Abyss, 
> - War of the Classes, and 
> - Before Adam.
>
> His most famous works include The Call of the Wild and White Fang, 
> both set in Alaska and the Yukon during the Klondike Gold Rush, 
> as well as the short stories "To Build a Fire", "An Odyssey of the North", and "Love of Life". 
> 
> He also wrote about the South Pacific in stories such as "The Pearls of Parlay" and "The Heathen".
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jack%20London)
