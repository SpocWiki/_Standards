## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> Paul Klee (German: [paʊ̯l ˈkleː]; 18 December 1879 – 29 June 1940) 
> was a Swiss-born German artist. 
> His highly individual style was influenced by movements in art 
> that included expressionism, cubism, and surrealism.
>
> Klee was a natural draftsman who experimented with 
> and eventually deeply explored color theory, writing about it extensively. 
> 
> His lectures Writings on Form and Design Theory 
> (Schriften zur Form und Gestaltungslehre), 
> published in English as the Paul Klee Notebooks, 
> are held to be as important for modern art 
> as Leonardo da Vinci's A Treatise on Painting was for the Renaissance.
>
> He and his colleague, Russian painter Wassily Kandinsky, 
> both taught at the Bauhaus school of art, design and architecture in Germany. 
> 
> His works reflect his dry humor and his sometimes childlike perspective, 
> his personal moods and beliefs, and his musicality.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul%20Klee)
