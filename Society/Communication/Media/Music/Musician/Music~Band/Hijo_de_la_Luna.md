# [[Hijo_de_la_Luna]] 

#is_/same_as :: [[WD~Hijo_de_la_Luna,690024]] 

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> "**Hijo de la luna**" (Spanish for 'Son of the Moon') 
> is a song written by José María Cano performed originally 
> by the Spanish band Mecano with lead singer Ana Torroja. 
> 
> It appeared on their 1986 album, Entre el cielo y el suelo, 
> and had great success all over the Spanish-speaking world, as did the album. 
> 
> From 28 December 1998 to 16 January 1999, 
> a cover version by Loona topped the German charts, 
> and reached number two on the Swiss singles' chart.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hijo%20de%20la%20Luna)  


## #has_/text_of_/lyrics 

The lyrics depict a [tragic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tragedy "Tragedy") [Romani legend](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romani_folklore "Romani folklore") of a [Roma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romani_people "Romani people") woman 
who awakes at [sunrise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunrise "Sunrise"), begging to the [moon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moon "Moon") 
to marry a Roma man ([calé](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cal%C3%A9 "Calé")) the same day. 
In exchange the moon asks for the woman's first-born as payment.

After the child is born, the woman and man are dismayed 
to see that his skin is the whitest white and his eyes are grey, 
even though they both have dark skin and dark eyes. 

The lyrics explain that the child is an [albino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albinism "Albinism"), due to being a son of the moon. 
But the man presumes that the father is a [payo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gadjo_\(non-Romani\) "Gadjo (non-Romani)") (a non-Roma person) 
and furiously [stabs his wife to death](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uxoricide "Uxoricide"). 
He takes the child and [abandons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Child_abandonment "Child abandonment") him on a [mountain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mountain "Mountain"), 
leaving the child to die of exposure.

Later the baby is said to live happily ever after with the Moon as his mother. 
When the child cries, the Moon [wanes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waning_Moon "Waning Moon") to cradle and comfort him.
