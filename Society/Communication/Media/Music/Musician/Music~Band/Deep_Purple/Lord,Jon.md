## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> John Douglas "Jon" Lord (9 June 1941 – 16 July 2012) was an English keyboardist and composer. 
> In 1968, Lord co-founded the hard rock band Deep Purple. 
> Lord performed on most of the band's most popular songs; 
> he and drummer Ian Paice were the only continuous members in the band between 1968 and 1976, 
> and also from its revival in 1984 until his retirement in 2002. 
> 
> He also played for the bands Whitesnake, Paice Ashton Lord, the Artwoods, the Flower Pot Men and Santa Barbara Machine Head. 
>
> Lord became known for his pioneering work in fusing rock with classical or baroque forms, 
> especially with Deep Purple. 
> 
> His distinctive organ playing during Deep Purple's hard rock period 
> was essential to the band's signature heavy sound 
> and contributed to the early development of heavy metal. 
> 
> On 11 November 2010, he was inducted as an Honorary Fellow of Stevenson College in Edinburgh, Scotland. 
> On 15 July 2011, he was awarded an honorary Doctor of Music degree at De Montfort Hall by the University of Leicester. 
> 
> Lord was posthumously inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame on 8 April 2016 
> as a member of Deep Purple.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jon%20Lord)
