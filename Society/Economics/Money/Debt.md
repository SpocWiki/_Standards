# [[Debt]] 

#is_/same_as :: [[../../../../../WikiData/WD~Debt,3196867|WD~Debt,3196867]] 

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Debt** is an obligation that requires one party, the debtor, 
> to pay money borrowed or otherwise withheld from another party, the creditor. 
> 
> Debt may be owed by a sovereign state or country, local government, company, or an individual. 
> Commercial debt is generally subject to contractual terms 
> regarding the amount and timing of repayments of principal and interest. 
> Loans, bonds, notes, and mortgages are all types of debt. 
> In financial accounting, debt is a type of financial transaction, as distinct from equity.
>
> The term can also be used metaphorically 
> to cover moral obligations and other interactions not based on a monetary value. 
> For example, in Western cultures, a person who has been helped by a second person 
> is sometimes said to owe a "debt of gratitude" to the second person.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Debt)
