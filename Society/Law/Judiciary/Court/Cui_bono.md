## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **cui bono**? (Classical Latin: [kui̯ ˈbɔnoː]), in English  "to whom is it a benefit?", 
> is a Latin phrase about identifying crime suspects. 
> 
> It depends on the fact that crimes are often committed to benefit their perpetrators; especially financially.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cui%20bono?) 

Cicero attributes this question to Lucius Cassius.
