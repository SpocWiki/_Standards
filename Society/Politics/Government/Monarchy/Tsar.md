## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Tsar** (also spelled czar, tzar, or csar; Bulgarian: цар, romanized: tsar; Russian: царь, romanized: tsar'; Serbian: цар, car) 
> is a title historically used by Slavic monarchs. 
> 
> The term is derived from the Latin word caesar, 
> which was intended to mean emperor in the European medieval sense of the term—
> a ruler with the same rank as a Roman emperor, 
> holding it by the approval of another emperor or a supreme ecclesiastical official—
> but was usually considered by Western Europeans to be equivalent to "king".
>
> Tsar and its variants were the official titles in the 
> - First Bulgarian Empire (681–1018), 
> - Second Bulgarian Empire (1185–1396), the 
> - Serbian Empire (1346–1371), and the 
> - Tsardom of Russia (1547–1721).
> - Kingdom of Bulgaria (1908–1946) 
>
> The first ruler to adopt the title tsar was Simeon I of Bulgaria. 
> Simeon II, the last tsar of Bulgaria, is the last person to have held this title.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tsar)
