## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> In sociology, social action, also known as Weberian social action, 
> is an act which takes into account the actions and reactions of individuals (or 'agents'). 
> 
> According to Max Weber, "Action is 'social' insofar as 
> its subjective meaning takes account of the behavior of others 
> and is thereby oriented in its course."
>
