## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Etiquette** (/ˈɛtikɛt, -kɪt/) can be defined as a set of norms of personal behavior in polite society, 
> usually occurring in the form of an ethical code of the expected and accepted social behaviors 
> that accord with the conventions and norms observed and practiced by a society, 
> a social class, or a social group. 
> 
> In modern English usage, the French word étiquette (label and tag) dates from the year 1750 
> and also originates from the French word for "ticket," 
> possibly symbolizing a person’s entry into society through proper behavior. 
> 
> There are many important historical figures that have helped to shape the meaning of the term 
> as well as provide varying perspectives.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Etiquette)
